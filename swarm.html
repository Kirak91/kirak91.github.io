Background

When it comes to collective payoffs, groups are more accurate when relying on independet though less accurate information sources. This phenomenon could already been shown in an experiment on news consumption (Pescetelli et al., 2020). This phenomenon is based on a statistical theorem, the condorcet jury theorem, which states that under certain circumstances, groups are more accurate if they are voting indpedenent from each other. Social Media Platforms, like twitter, are able to polarize followers, but also give room for everyone to state their opinions.

Task

We conducted a behavioral group study to test if people are changing their strategies in information seeking in groups depending on how they are recinforced in the context of social media platforms.

My resposibilities

I took the lead on this project under Dr. Pescetelli's supervision. I collected the data for this group experimenmt through Prolific and I am currently analyzing the data, by using R and Python. I am mostly using mixed and logistic regression models.
