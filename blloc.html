Background

Task

Technology should bend to fit the user and not the other way around. I was disappointed to see that smartphone companies are using their psychological expertise, to make a device that make the user's so addicted. Nowadays, many people can't leave their home without their smartphones and are more subsceptible for mental disorders, like depression or/and anxiety. I wanted to contribute to a smartphone design, that makes people engage with their friends and family during dinner instead of their displays and that makes you productive instead of distracted. For this project I joined a start-up in Berlin called "Blloc", that develops an alternative useer interface for android phones, called "Ratio". The company also previously released their own smartphone, that focused on principles on mindfulness.

My resposibilities

When putting such a unique idea into practice, it is important to find out what the users are actually looking for.

Results

The results were counterintuitive to our hypothesis. Introducing bots to a social learning test in transmission chains did not improve the performance of the players in the long run. While humans copied from bots, they did so at a lower rate than copying humans with the same performance. We found, that Although we did not find the expected results this research sheds light to a new area of investigation which is currently relatively unexplored: hybrid social learning.
