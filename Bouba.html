Past Project

Background

The bouba/kiki phenomenon represents a non-arbitrary mapping between human language and shapes. While the sound "kiki" is associated with sharp objects, the sound "bouba" is more often associated with round objects. We wanted to see whether these mental representations had an influence on memory.

Task

We planned an experiment by pairing stimuli of the bouba/kiki effect with colors to test if these astract attachements could consistently enhance memory. The project was later presented as a poster presentation at the Society for Neuroscience Conference in Chicago (2019).

My resposibilities

I was involved in study and stimuli design. I helped set up the study and carried out part of the data collection.

Results

We could see, that the bouba/kiki effect could enhance memory.
