Background

Using questionnaires to answer clinical questions about mental disorders is often not reliable and it only works retrospectively. For many disorders an early onset of therapy is a good predictor for outcome of the disorder and likelihood of relapses. Passive sensing is a method of collecting data (digital phenotypes), like sleeping patterns, heartrate, walking patterns or phone calls, from mobile phones or wearables. With these large behavioral data sets, machine learning algorithms can be used to predict mental disorders long before the onset of the disorder.

Task

Our team is working on a large longitudinal study, to study the effects of smartphone supported interventions for participants with mental disorders. Within this study, we want to test, whether passive sensing can support the outcome of this hybrid therapy format.

My resposibilities

We are currently at the beginning of the study. I am involved in literature research on passive sensing and mental illnesses. The next steps will be to look for a specific research question and to design an plan the study.
